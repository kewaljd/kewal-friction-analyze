📘 Project Details

🎯 Aim

To analyze the behavior of objects moving on an inclined plane by studying the effect of friction, angle of inclination, surface characteristics, and object geometry on motion parameters such as velocity and acceleration.

📖 Theory

When an object is placed on an inclined plane, the gravitational force acting on it can be resolved into two components: one parallel to the plane (Mg sinθ), which tends to move the object downward, and one perpendicular to the plane (Mg cosθ), which gives rise to the normal reaction force (N). The frictional force (f = μN) opposes motion and depends on the nature of the surface.

If the component of gravitational force along the plane exceeds the maximum static friction, the object begins to move, after which kinetic friction governs the motion. The net acceleration of the object is given by:

a = g (sinθ − μcosθ)

📐 Analysis

The simulation demonstrates that at lower angles, frictional force dominates and prevents motion. As the angle increases, the component of gravity along the plane increases, eventually overcoming friction and initiating motion. Different surfaces exhibit different coefficients of friction, directly affecting the rate of acceleration and velocity of the object.

🧪 Observations

Experimental observations show that smoother surfaces (lower μ) result in higher velocities, while rough surfaces (higher μ) resist motion. The type of object affects rotational behavior (e.g., sphere vs cube), but the fundamental translational motion depends primarily on friction and angle.

📊 Conclusion

The study confirms that friction plays a critical role in controlling motion on inclined planes. The balance between gravitational force and friction determines whether an object remains at rest or accelerates. This principle is widely applicable in engineering systems such as braking mechanisms, conveyor systems, and slope stability analysis.
